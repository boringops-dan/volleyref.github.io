What Referees Say

Why VolleyStation Score Earns 2.7 Stars.

VolleyStation Score has institutional backing from FIVB and Volleyball World. But the user experience tells a different story.

"Rosters Are Never Correct"

VolleyStation Score imports rosters from AES tournament software. When the AES data has errors -- wrong names, wrong jersey numbers -- the app inherits them. This is the single most common complaint in reviews. VolleyRef lets you enter team info directly, so what you type is what you get.

"Paper Is Way Easier Until You Watch the Tutorial"

Multiple VolleyStation users report that the app requires watching tutorial videos before a tournament to understand how it works. A tool for real-time officiating should not require prep work. VolleyRef is designed so a referee who has never seen it can score a full match without reading anything.

Useless Without AES

VolleyStation Score is built to integrate with AES tournament management software. If you're not running an AES-managed tournament, the roster import that the app is built around doesn't apply. Most referees work far more non-AES matches than AES ones. For those matches, you need a standalone app.

TL;DR

VolleyStation Score is the right tool when your tournament requires it and your AES data is clean. For every other match -- club volleyball, high school, rec leagues, beach, open tournaments -- VolleyRef.App is the better choice: easier to learn, works standalone, includes full undo, and lets spectators follow the score.

To Be Fair

When VolleyStation Score Is the Right Tool.

AES-Managed Tournaments

If your tournament organization requires VolleyStation Score and manages rosters through AES, use VolleyStation. The roster-sync and division-ranking integration is genuinely useful when the data is clean and the organization mandates it.

Free for Org-Mandated Use

If your USAV region or tournament provides VolleyStation at no extra cost and assigns you to use it, there's no out-of-pocket cost. VolleyRef requires a subscription for matches beyond the first 3.
