VolleyRef vs iScore Volleyball.

Both are popular volleyball apps. They do very different things. Heres how to figure out which one is right for your situation.

Rule Enforcement Platform Support Pricing Live Scoreboards

TL;DR

iScore is a coach stats tool for iOS. VolleyRef is a referee tool for any device. If you need to enforce rules, track liberos, or share a live scoreboard, use VolleyRef. If you want to track kills and digs on an iPhone, use iScore.

VolleyRef.App

Built for Referees.

VolleyRef is designed to run a match, not just record what happened. The difference matters when youre on the court.

Rules Are Enforced, Not Just Tracked

The app knows FIVB, USAV, NFHS, and NCAA rules and applies them automatically. Try to make an illegal substitution and it blocks you. Try to call a timeout you dont have and it stops you. You cant accidentally break the rules.

Works on Any Device

Its a web app. Open it in any browser on any phone, tablet, or laptop. No app store install required. Android, iOS, Windows, Mac -- doesnt matter. iScore requires an iPhone or iPad.

Live Scoreboard Sharing

One tap generates a shareable link. Parents in the stands can follow along on their phones. Cast it to a TV or projector courtside. iScore has no equivalent feature.

Two-Referee Support

Run a second referee on a separate device. Both see the same match in real time. iScore is single-device only.

Full Undo/Redo

Made a mistake? Undo every action, including substitutions, timeouts, and score changes. Not just the last point -- every single action has a complete undo history.

PDF Scoresheet Export

Clean, professional scoresheet at the end of every match. Send it to the tournament director or keep it for your records.

Not a Stats App

VolleyRef doesnt track kills, digs, hitting percentage, or serve zones. Its focused on officiating. If you need coaching analytics, iScore or VBStats will serve you better.

Pricing: First 3 matches are free, no credit card needed. After that: $5/week (great for tournament weekends), $15/month (league season), or $50/year (best value for year-round referees).

iScore Volleyball

Built for Coaches.

iScore does one thing really well: detailed player statistics on iOS. If thats what you need, its a good app.

Detailed Player Stats

Track kills, assists, blocks, digs, and hitting percentage. Good for coaches who want player performance data over the course of a season.

One-Time Purchase

$10 and you own it forever. No subscription. Good value if you use it year-round.

iOS Only

No Android version, no web version. If you dont have an iPhone or iPad, iScore isnt an option.

No Rule Enforcement

iScore records what you tell it. It wont stop you from making an illegal substitution, calling an extra timeout, or letting a libero serve when they shouldnt. You have to know the rules yourself.

No Live Scoreboard Sharing

No way to share the score in real time with spectators. Youre the only one who can see whats on your screen.

Pricing: $10 one-time on the App Store. Best for: Coaches on iOS who want detailed player statistics and dont need rule enforcement or live scoreboard sharing.

Bottom Line

Which One Is Right for You?

  • Dont have an iPhone or want to use any device

  • Are a coach tracking player performance stats
  • Want to analyze kills, digs, and hitting percentage
  • Are on iOS and prefer a one-time purchase
  • Dont need the app to enforce volleyball rules
