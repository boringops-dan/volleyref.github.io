Why BeachTennisRef exists

Beach tennis has distinct rules from regular tennis -- no-advantage scoring, different tie-break serving (ITF Rule 8b), specific match formats, and its own serve rotation. Generic tennis apps that include a "beach tennis mode" handle the score format but ignore the rules underneath. BeachTennisRef is the first app built from the ground up for beach tennis officiating.

Why Generic Tennis Apps Fall Short.

Several tennis apps include a "beach tennis mode." Here's what they get wrong.

Wrong Tie-Break Serving

ITF Rule 8b specifies that in a tie-break, the first point is served by the player whose turn it is, then serve alternates every two points. Generic tennis apps use standard tennis tie-break logic (7-point, different alternation pattern), which is incorrect for beach tennis.

No Side-Switch Logic

Beach tennis changes ends at specific intervals during play. Generic apps track the score but don't know when ends should change or prompt the referee. BeachTennisRef tracks and signals ends changes automatically per ITF format.

No Hindrance and Let Tracking

Beach tennis has specific let and hindrance rules. Generic tennis apps don't handle these -- they're not part of standard tennis. BeachTennisRef includes let calls and hindrance actions as first-class events that are logged and undoable.

iOS Only or No Offline

The most capable generic option (Tennis Scoreboard) is iOS only with no documented offline support. Beach courts often have poor cell coverage. BeachTennisRef works fully offline on any device.
