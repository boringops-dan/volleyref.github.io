# Volleyball Rotation Rules Explained

Volleyball rotation rules govern when and how players change positions during a match. Under FIVB Rule 7.6.1, teams rotate clockwise when they win the serve from the opponent (sideout). The player in position 2 moves to position 1 and becomes the server.

## Key Rotation Facts

- **6 positions** numbered 1-6, with position 1 at the serving position (right back).
- **Rotate on sideout only**: the serving team never rotates when it wins a rally.
- **Clockwise direction**: per FIVB Rule 7.6.1.
- **Positional order at serve**: per FIVB Rule 7.5, all players must be in correct rotational positions at the moment of the serve. Violations are overlap faults.

## Rotation Fault (FIVB Rule 7.7)

Serving out of the correct order results in: a point for the opponent, the serve for the opponent, and cancellation of all points scored by the faulty team since the error.

## Overlap Fault (FIVB Rule 7.5.2)

At the moment of serve, players must be in rotational order. Right-side players must be right of center, left-side players left of center, and front-row players closer to the net than their back-row counterpart on the same side. Violation = point and serve to opponent.

## Front Row vs Back Row

- **Front row (positions 2, 3, 4):** may attack and block.
- **Back row (positions 1, 5, 6):** may not block; may not attack above net height from in front of the 3-meter line (FIVB Rule 13.3.2).
