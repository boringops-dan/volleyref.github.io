Volleyball Rules Q&A

What is a screening violation in volleyball?

A screening violation occurs when players on the serving team obstruct the receiving team's view of the server or the ball's trajectory at the moment of serve. It is a fault that awards a point and serve to the opponent.

Full Explanation

Per FIVB Rule 12.5.1, the serving team must not screen the opponents from seeing the server or the trajectory of the served ball. Actions such as players jumping, waving their arms, or standing together to form a visual barrier are screening faults. Individual players standing normally in their legal rotational positions are not considered to be screening even if they happen to be near the server's path. A screening fault results in a point and serve for the receiving team. Under NFHS Rule 6-2, similar screening rules apply. NCAA rules also prohibit screening.

Rulesets covered: FIVB/USAV/NFHS/NCAA  |  Rule Reference: FIVB Rule 12.5.1; NFHS Rule 6-2
