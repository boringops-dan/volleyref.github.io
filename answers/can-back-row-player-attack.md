# Can a back-row player attack the ball in volleyball?

**Short Answer:** A back-row player may attack the ball from behind the 3-meter attack line. They may not complete an attack hit where the ball is entirely above the net if they took off from at or in front of the line.

## Full Explanation

Per FIVB Rule 13.3.2, a back-row attack fault occurs when a back-row player (positions 1, 5, or 6) completes an attack hit while: (1) the ball is entirely above the height of the net, AND (2) the player's takeoff (jump) occurred from at or in front of the attack line (3-meter line). A back-row player may jump from behind the attack line, hit the ball above the net, and land in front of the line -- that is legal, because the takeoff was from behind the line. A back-row player may also attack below net height from anywhere. The libero faces a stricter rule: no attack hit above net height from any position (FIVB Rule 19.3.1.3).

**Rulesets:** FIVB
**Rule Reference:** FIVB Rule 13.3.2
