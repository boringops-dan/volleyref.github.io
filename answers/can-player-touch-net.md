# Can a player touch the net during volleyball play?

**Short Answer:** No. Touching the net between the antennas during an action of playing the ball is a fault. Incidental contact that does not affect play (such as contact outside the antennas or contact by a player's hair) is not called.

## Full Explanation

Per FIVB Rule 11.3.1, a player faults if they touch the net or antenna between the antennas during an action of playing the ball. The fault results in a point for the opponent. However, the rule is narrower than commonly assumed: contact with the net cables, posts, or the net outside the antenna does not constitute a net fault. Also, incidental contact with the net by hair or parts of the body that do not interfere with play is not called as a fault per FIVB Rule 11.3.1 guidance. Practically, referees call net touches during blocking, attacking, and plays near the net. NFHS and NCAA follow the same basic principle.

**Rulesets:** FIVB/USAV/NFHS/NCAA
**Rule Reference:** FIVB Rule 11.3.1
