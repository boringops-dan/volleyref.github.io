# What is the libero rule in high school (NFHS) volleyball?

**Short Answer:** Under NFHS rules, the libero may serve in one rotational position per set (unlike FIVB/NCAA where serving is prohibited). All other standard libero restrictions apply (no attacking above net height, no blocking, no front-row play).

## Full Explanation

Under NFHS volleyball rules, the libero is allowed to serve, but only from one specific rotational position per set. This means the libero may replace the player in that one position when it is time to serve, serve the ball, and then be replaced before the rally begins, or the libero may remain and play defense. This is the most significant difference from FIVB Rule 19.3.1.1 and NCAA rules, which prohibit libero serving entirely. Under all rulesets including NFHS, the libero may not attack above net height, may not block, may not set overhand from in front of the attack line for an above-net attack, and must wear a contrasting jersey. NFHS libero replacements also occur in the replacement zone and are unlimited per set.

**Rulesets:** NFHS
**Rule Reference:** NFHS Rule 12 (Libero)
