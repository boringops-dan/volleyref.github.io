Detailed Breakdown

Every App, Honestly Reviewed.

1

BeachTennisRef.App -- Best for Officials and Serious Players

Platform: Any device (web app, no install required)

Pricing: Free to start, then $5/week, $15/month, or $50/year

ITF Rules: Full enforcement

Undo: Yes -- every action

Offline: Yes

Spectator sharing: Yes -- any-device link

Best for: Referees, tournament play, anyone who needs correct rule enforcement

Not ideal for: Completely free casual play (first matches free, subscription after)

BeachTennisRef.App is the only app designed specifically for beach tennis. It enforces ITF rules end-to-end: no-advantage scoring, correct serve rotation per set, tie-break serving per Rule 8b (alternating every two points after the first), ends changes, and hindrance/let calls as first-class events. This is not a tennis app with a beach mode -- it's a beach tennis app.

The undo feature deserves specific mention: first server selection, point calls, let calls, and serve changes are all undoable. In tournament play, the ability to immediately correct a mis-tapped point without a match dispute is worth the price of admission on its own.

Works fully offline, which matters for outdoor courts with inconsistent cell coverage. Generates a shareable live link that spectators open on any device -- no app install on their end.

2

Tennis Scoreboard -- Best iOS-Only Casual Option

Platform: iOS only (iPhone and iPad)

Pricing: Free basic, $9.99 one-time PRO unlock

ITF Rules: Format only -- score structure, not rule enforcement

Undo: Yes -- one-tap undo

Offline: Not documented

Spectator sharing: Yes -- shareable score link

Best for: iPhone users who want a simple score tracker for casual beach tennis

Not ideal for: Android users, official referees, anyone needing ITF rule enforcement

Tennis Scoreboard is the most capable general-purpose tennis app that includes a beach tennis mode. The PRO version adds an Apple Watch companion and more format options. Its beach tennis mode sets the correct score structure: 1 set, 4 games to win, 4-4 tie-break. One-tap undo and a shareable score link are genuine strengths.

What it doesn't do: enforce the rules beneath that structure. The app won't alert you to a wrong server, won't apply tie-break Rule 8b alternation, and won't track when ends should change. For casual play where everyone knows the rules, that's fine. For a referee who needs the app to catch errors, it's not sufficient.

iOS only is the other hard limit. Android players and referees have no path to this app.

3

Referi -- Best for Club Players Who Want a Voice Umpire

Platform: iOS and Android

Pricing: Free app; hardware controllers sold separately

ITF Rules: None

Undo: Long press on hardware button

Offline: Not documented

Spectator sharing: TV mirroring via WiFi

Best for: Club players and coaches who want a virtual umpire voice and hands-free hardware buttons

Not ideal for: Official referees, tournaments, anyone needing ITF rule enforcement

Referi's differentiator is its virtual umpire voice that announces scores, and optional Bluetooth hardware controllers (ReTouch buttons) that let you score without touching your phone. For club practice sessions and recreational play where the umpire-call atmosphere adds to the experience, it's a fun product.

Referi is primarily focused on padel and tennis. Beach tennis is adjacent but not a named format. There is no ITF rule enforcement, no beach-tennis-specific serve rotation, and no tie-break logic. The TV mirroring feature requires a WiFi connection to a display device -- it's not a shareable link.

Not a tool for official beach tennis refereeing. A good option for club players who prioritize the experience over rule accuracy.

4

Generic Score Counters -- When You Just Need a Number

Apps like KeepTheScore, simple tally counters, or spreadsheets can track beach tennis points if all you need is "what's the score" with no rule awareness. They won't know the format, won't track serves, won't know when to change ends, and have no undo tied to match context.

Appropriate for: pickup games where everyone already knows the rules and nobody disputes anything. Not appropriate for: any match where the result matters.
