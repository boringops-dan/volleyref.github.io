When Running From Memory Works.

The honest case for the tool-free approach. There is one, and its valid in specific settings.

Zero Setup Friction

Kids show up for scrimmage, whistle goes, you start. No device to unlock, no match to configure, no roster to enter. Pure start.

Full Attention on the Court

You are watching the players, not a screen. For a veteran who can hold the counters in their head, that is a real advantage on calls.

Fine for Casual Play

Scrimmages, drop-in, clinics, coaching skill drills. When no official record is needed and nothing is on the line, the tool-free approach fits.

No Tech Dependency

No battery, no signal, no screen. The match can run in any gym in any state.

The Limits

Where Your Brain Runs Out.

These are predictable, not rare. Every experienced ref has had at least three of these moments.

The Timeout You Forgot.

Coach calls for a timeout, you grant it. Two rotations later a player calls for another. Was that the second or the third? Nobody remembers with confidence. You guess.

The Score Slip.

Long rally, controversial call, both benches yelling. When you call the next score, you are off by one. No record means no correction, just a louder coach.

Set Three of Match Four.

You have worked six hours, officiated fifteen sets, called hundreds of rallies. Your attention is thin. Thats the exact moment mental scorekeeping breaks, and thats when the tight matches happen at a tournament.

The Coach Appeal.

A coach disputes a score from two rallies back. You have no log. You can say what you remember, but your memory is not evidence. The call comes down to tone, not truth.

Nothing to Show the Director.

Tournament director walks up between matches: how did that one go? You have a score. Thats it. No sub info, no timeout record, no injury pause log. Nothing to back you up if a parent files a complaint.

You Are the Single Point of Failure.

You sneeze, you blink at the wrong moment, you misheard the coach. Nothing else is tracking. One missed input is a scoring error with no detection and no recovery.
