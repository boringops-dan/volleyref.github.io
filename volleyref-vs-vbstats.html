VolleyRef vs VBStats.

Both track what happens in a volleyball match. They serve completely different roles. Heres how to figure out which one belongs on your sideline.

Rule Enforcement Platform Support Pricing Player Analytics

TL;DR

VBStats is a coach analytics tool for tracking kills, digs, and player performance. VolleyRef is a referee tool that enforces rules automatically. If you need to officiate a match correctly, use VolleyRef. If you want to analyze player stats after the fact, VBStats is built for that.

VolleyRef.App

Built for Referees.

VolleyRef is designed to run a match correctly, not just record numbers. When youre the official on court, that distinction matters.

Rules Are Enforced, Not Just Tracked

The app knows FIVB, USAV, NFHS, and NCAA rules and applies them during the match. Try to make an illegal substitution and it blocks you. Exceed your timeout limit and it stops you. You cant accidentally break the rules.

Works on Any Device

Its a web app. Open it in any browser on any phone, tablet, or laptop. No app install required. Android, iOS, Windows, Mac -- doesnt matter. VBStats requires installing an app on iOS or Android.

Live Scoreboard Sharing

One tap generates a shareable link. Parents and spectators can follow along on their phones. Cast it to a TV or projector courtside. VBStats has no equivalent feature.

Two-Referee Support

Run a second referee on a separate device. Both see the same match in real time, synced live. VBStats is a single-device tool -- no real-time multi-device support.

Full Undo/Redo

Made a mistake? Undo every action -- substitutions, timeouts, score changes. Not just the last point. Every single action has a complete undo history so you can correct errors without losing the match.

Official PDF Scoresheet

Clean, professional scoresheet at the end of every match. Includes set scores, substitution records, and timeout logs. Send it to the tournament director or keep it for your records.

Not a Stats App

VolleyRef doesnt track kills, digs, hitting percentage, or serve zones. Its focused on running the match correctly. If deep player analytics is your primary need, VBStats or iScore will serve you better for that specific job.

Pricing: First 3 matches are free, no credit card needed. After that: $5/week (great for tournament weekends), $15/month (league season), or $50/year (best value for year-round referees).

VBStats

Built for Coaches.

VBStats is the most detailed volleyball statistics app available. If your job is player development and performance analysis, its hard to beat.

Deep Player Analytics

Track kills, digs, blocks, assists, aces, errors, hitting percentage, serve zones, attack zones, and landing zones by player. VBStats gives coaches data that translates directly into practice plans.

iOS and Android

Available on both major mobile platforms. More flexible than iScore which is iOS only. Still requires the app to be installed -- no browser access.

Steep Learning Curve

The depth that makes VBStats powerful also makes it complex. Tracking every stat category during a live match requires a dedicated stats operator. Its not designed for a single referee keeping score and officiating simultaneously.

No Rule Enforcement

VBStats records what you tell it. It wont stop you from making an illegal substitution, calling an extra timeout, or violating libero rules. You need to know the rules yourself and enter data correctly.

No Live Scoreboard Sharing

No way to share the score with spectators in real time. No shareable links, no cast-to-TV feature. VBStats is focused on data collection, not display.

Pricing: VBStats has a free tier with basic features and paid subscriptions for advanced analytics access. Best for: Coaches and stats operators on iOS or Android who need deep player performance analytics and have a dedicated person to track stats.
