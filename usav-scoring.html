Rule Enforcement

Every USAV Rule. Enforced.

You pick USAV. The app handles the rest. No rulebook lookup. No second-guessing.

18 per team, per set. Three times the FIVB limit. You lose count at sub number 12. The app doesn't. Try to record the 19th — blocked.

USAV lets the libero serve — but only in one rotational position. The app knows which one. Try to serve from the wrong spot and it stops you.

Player gets hurt. Player gets DQ'd. USAV has its own exceptional sub procedure — and it's not the same as FIVB. The app walks you through every step so you don't have to dig through the casebook mid-match.

Two timeouts per set, 30 seconds each. The app tracks how many are left and won't let you record a third. Simple — until you forget in the heat of a 24-22 set.

Sub limits are the obvious difference. But libero replacements, sanctions, and match procedures all have subtle USAV-specific wrinkles. Select USAV as your ruleset and every one of those differences is handled automatically.

USAV Is Not FIVB.

Club volleyball. Regional qualifiers. Adult leagues. If you're refereeing in the United States, you're running USAV rules. And those are not the same as FIVB. The sub limit alone — 18 vs. 6 — changes everything about how you manage a set. Coaches use all 18. You need to track every one.
