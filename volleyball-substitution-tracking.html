The Problem

Lose Count Once. That's All It Takes.

Fifth Sub or Sixth?

Three subs came in at once. You think that was five total. Maybe six. The coach thinks it was four. You're both wrong. And now you're arguing about it while the server is waiting.

DQ. Now What?

A player gets disqualified. Exceptional substitution rules kick in. But which rules? FIVB and USAV handle it differently. Your rulebook is in your bag. The coach is in your face.

Six Changes. One Dead Ball.

Both teams want to sub. Three in, three out. Each side. That's six changes to track, six limits to check, and zero time to do the math. Miss one and you've got a protest.

How It Works

Every Sub. Every Limit. Every Ruleset.

Per-Set Limits by Ruleset

FIVB: 6 per set. USAV: 18 per set. NFHS: its own set of rules. The app tracks the count and blocks any sub that would exceed the limit. You never count in your head again.

Exceptional Subs, Handled

Player injured. Player disqualified. The exceptional sub rules kick in — and they're different under FIVB and USAV. The app knows which ruleset you picked. It applies the right rules, determines who can replace whom, and tells you if it counts against your limit.

Three Subs at Once? No Problem.

Batch entry. Record multiple subs between rallies without falling behind the pace of play. The app checks every one against the limits before recording.
