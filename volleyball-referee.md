# Volleyball Referee Guide

Volleyball referees are responsible for ensuring fair play according to the applicable ruleset (FIVB, USAV, NFHS, or NCAA).

## Referee Positions

- **First referee (R1):** stands on a platform at one end of the net; has authority over all officials; makes final decisions.
- **Second referee (R2):** stands on the floor at the opposite end of the net; assists R1; watches net, centerline, and substitutions.
- **Scorer:** records all game events on the scoresheet; tracks substitutions, timeouts, libero, and rotations.
- **Assistant scorer (Libero tracker):** tracks libero replacements.
- **Line judges (2 or 4):** signal ball in/out, touches, and antenna violations.

## Key Referee Signals

Referees use standardized hand signals for: point, service, service fault, rotation fault, positional fault, substitution, timeout, end of set, and more. FIVB official signals are published in the FIVB Rules of the Game.

## Common Calls

- **Rotation fault:** wrong player serves -- per FIVB Rule 7.7.
- **Positional fault (overlap):** players out of rotational order at serve -- per FIVB Rule 7.5.
- **Ball in/out:** line judges signal, R1 confirms.
- **Net fault:** ball crosses under the net or outside the antenna.
- **Back-row attack:** per FIVB Rule 13.3.2.
- **Libero violation:** per FIVB Rule 19.3.1.
